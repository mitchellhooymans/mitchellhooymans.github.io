Reflections on My First Year as a PhD Student

Mitchell Hooymans
December 28, 2025
7 min read

One year ago, I embarked on a journey that would fundamentally change how I think about science, research, and myself. Starting a PhD is unlike anything I had experienced before, and as I look back on these first twelve months, I'm struck by how much I've learned—not just about astrophysics, but about the process of becoming a researcher.

The Excitement of Beginning

The first few weeks were a whirlwind of orientation sessions, meetings with supervisors, and trying to understand the scope of my research project. The impostor syndrome was real—surrounded by brilliant colleagues and wondering if I truly belonged.

Challenges and Growth

No PhD journey is without its obstacles. There were moments of frustration when code wouldn't work, when simulations failed, and when papers seemed impossible to finish. But each challenge taught me something valuable about persistence and problem-solving.

"The PhD is not just about the destination—it's about who you become along the way."

What I've Learned

  • Research rarely goes according to plan—and that's okay
  • Asking for help is a strength, not a weakness
  • Taking breaks is essential for creativity and mental health
  • The research community is supportive and welcoming
  • Every small step forward is progress worth celebrating

Looking Forward

As I begin my second year, I'm excited about the research directions opening up before me. The challenges ahead are daunting, but I feel better equipped to face them than I did a year ago.

Advice for New PhD Students

  1. Build relationships with your peers—they understand what you're going through
  2. Set realistic expectations for yourself
  3. Celebrate your wins, no matter how small
  4. Don't compare your journey to others
  5. Remember why you started

If you're considering a PhD or just starting one, feel free to reach out. I'm always happy to chat about the experience!

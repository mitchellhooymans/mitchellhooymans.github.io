The formation and dynamics of Stellar black holes in dense star clusters

My research investigates the interplay between stellar evolution and dynamics in dense star clusters. We explore how the evolution of massive stars and the formation of black holes influence the cluster's dynamical history. By combining rapid population synthesis with state of the art N-body simulations, we model the complete lifecycle of these systems to understand the origins of compact object mergers.

Gravitational Wave Physics

Complementing the dynamical studies, this research focuses on the gravitational waveforms emitted during the inspiral and ringdown phases of binary black hole mergers. By analyzing these signals, we can infer the properties of the progenitor black holes and test the predictions of General Relativity in the strong-field regime.

Key Areas: Waveform Analysis, General Relativity Testing, Signal Processing

Dark Sirens & The Hubble Tension

Using gravitational wave detections of binary black hole mergers—'dark sirens' without electromagnetic counterparts—to measure the expansion rate of the Universe. This method offers a novel, independent probe to help resolve the ongoing Hubble tension.

Key Areas: Cosmology, Bayesian Inference, Gravitational Wave Astronomy
